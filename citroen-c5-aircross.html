Gareth's Comments

The Citroen C5 Aircross represents a distinctly French approach to the family SUV, prioritizing comfort over sportiness. Its Progressive Hydraulic Cushion suspension is genuinely impressive, filtering out road imperfections that would unsettle many rivals. Combined with the Advanced Comfort seats, it provides one of the most relaxing driving experiences in its class.

For a driving pattern focused on weekend use, holidays and primarily motorway/A-road journeys, the C5 Aircross BlueHDI is extremely well-suited. The 1.5 BlueHDI engine delivers impressive real-world motorway economy of 48-52 mpg, while the 2.0 BlueHDI offers more power for those who regularly carry heavier loads or tow, with economy in the 45-48 mpg range. On long motorway stretches, the comfort-focused suspension and supportive seats really come into their own, reducing fatigue significantly. The sliding and reclining rear seats provide excellent flexibility for passengers or cargo on weekend trips and holidays.

The Flair trim offers the best balance of equipment and value with its additional safety features and reversing camera proving particularly useful for a vehicle of this size. While the C5 Aircross isn't the most engaging driver's car, its focus on comfort makes it ideal for those who prioritize a relaxed journey over cornering dynamics. For motorway cruising and longer A-road trips, it's one of the most comfortable options in its class, particularly with the 1.5 BlueHDI engine which provides adequate performance with excellent efficiency.
